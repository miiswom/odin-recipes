French Apple Tart

Description

Perfect treat after a Sunday dinner, the French Apple Tart (or 'Tarte Amandine aux Pommes' in French) is a classical of French cuisine.

The tart is made of apples and frangipane, and goes well after any meal to make all the family happy!

Ingredients

  • 1 pastry dough roll
  • ½ cup butter, softened
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • ⅔ cup ground almonds
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 medium sweet apples - peeled, cored, halved and thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar for decoration
  • ¼ cup apricot jelly

Steps

  1. Mix together the butter and 1/2 cup of sugar until light and soft.
  2. Gradually add in the egg and the egg yolk one at a time
  3. Pour in the lemon juice.
  4. Stir 2 tablespoons of flour into the ground almonds, then mix into the batter
  5. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  6. Spread the frangipane into an even layer in the pastry.
  7. Arrange the apple slices in an overlapping spiral pattern. Start at the outside edge, and work towards the center.
  8. Bake for 15 minutes, or until the filling begins to brown. Reduce the oven temperature to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  9. Bake for another 10 minutes, then sprinkle sugar over the top of the tart. Return to the oven for 10 more minutes, or until the sugar caramelizes slightly.
  10. Cool the tart on a wire rack. A short time before serving, warm the apricot jelly. Add some water if necessary to make it a liquid consistency. Brush onto the tart for a nice shine.
